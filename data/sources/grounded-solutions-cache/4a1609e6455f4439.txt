Tahoe, Calif., has long been known for its beaches, parks, resorts and entertainment. Now\u2014thanks to a public-private partnership between Grounded Solutions Network member St. Joseph Community Land Trust and Related California\u2014the city is also known for an intentionally planned, high-quality affordable housing community that will become home to hundreds of families.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">The emerging neighborhood, known as Sugar Pine Village, consists of 248 units on 10 acres of public land with access to transit and within walking distance of a grocery store and pharmacy. It was the first competitively awarded site made available under Governor Gavin Newsom\u2019s executive order to open state lands for affordable housing development, and it is the largest affordable housing project in South Lake Tahoe\u2019s history.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<h4 class=\"wp-block-heading has-orange-color has-text-color has-link-color wp-elements-cea30418961e099c431ceb2e8ef94f3f\"><strong>The Golden Opportunity<\/strong><\/h4>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">The decision to jointly pursue this competitive development opportunity came naturally to for-profit Related California and non-profit St. Joseph CLT who both saw several very clear mutual benefits to working together.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">\u201cWe\u2019re a small nonprofit with a very small staff. There&#8217;s no way we could do a project like this alone,\u201d said <strong>Jean Diaz, Saint Joseph CLT\u2019s Executive Director.<\/strong> \u201cWe felt that Related was the most logical partner for us given their experience and capacity.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">The Related Companies\u2014including Related California\u2014<a href=\"https:\/\/www.related.com\/affordable-housing\">manage more than 55,000 affordable residences throughout the country<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">For its part, Related was keenly aware of the importance of St. Joseph\u2019s established relationships within the South Lake Tahoe community and City government.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">\u201cFinding a local partner is important to us because there is that local expertise in knowing what the community needs. This is the story of intergovernmental participation.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">Another key component was California\u2019s passage of <a href=\"https:\/\/leginfo.legislature.ca.gov\/faces\/billNavClient.xhtml?bill_id=201720180SB35\">SB 35<\/a> in 2017, a law that allows for by-right approval\u2014a much more streamlined approval process\u2014of affordable housing projects that meet certain criteria.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">\u201cSB 35 is a revolutionary tool for entitling affordable housing in California,\u201d Hanson said. \u201cThe entitlement of the entire project took six months. Without SB 35 and other favorable factors, this could have been a five-to-six-year project, if it succeeded at all.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">Because Tahoe is surrounded by mountains \u2014 the Sierra Nevada mountains to the west and the Carson range to the east \u2014 construction is strictly prohibited from October to May each year to prevent runoff.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">\u201cThe phasing was due to the construction timeline and also because of the financing environment that we had to work our way through,\u201d Hanson said. \u201cThe first two phases had set-aside funding from the state, and then the City of South Lake Tahoe also provided funding.\u201d<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">Sugar Pine\u2019s first two phases have already been completed. Here in Washington state, our affiliate serves both King and Kittitas Counties. In my role as the Associate Director of Homeowner Services, I oversee and support the team that works with our homebuyers from outreach, through application, eligibility screening, selection, education and preparation, to closing. Prior to this role (which is in all earnestness, my dream job), I\u2019d previously been a residential real estate agent. Helping my first time buyer clients navigate the aggressive competition of the housing market during the early pandemic years was both rewarding and demoralizing, as I felt powerless against the inequity of a system where only the highest bidder prevails. While still working as an agent, I went back to grad school for a Master\u2019s in Urban Design and Planning with a focus on affordable housing, and shortly after graduating, began my role at Habitat.&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\"><strong>What does \u201chousing that lasts for generations\u201d mean to you?<\/strong>&nbsp;<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">Habitat\u2019s permanently affordable housing opportunities are made possible through the use of a community land trust model. The Mueller Foundation also offers some of its homeownership opportunities to households making up to 120 percent of MFI (currently about $160,000 for a four-person household).<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\"><strong>How It All Came Together<\/strong><\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">Redeveloping 700 acres of city-owned land just minutes from downtown was a rare opportunity. The Foundation shifted to a restrictive covenant approach with a fixed two percent annual appreciation cap. It\u2019s a more sustainable structure for retaining homes for future homeowners, and with over 200 homes resold to other affordable owners, clearly, it\u2019s working.<\/p>\n\n\n\n<p class=\"\">The shared equity model has evolved to help the Foundation preserve long-term affordability and sustainability as families move, grow, or experience financial changes.
